# i18n glossary proposals — UX-research terminology

**Status: PROPOSALS for native-reviewer steer. Nothing here is applied.** Generated 30 Jun 2026 from a 10-language research fan-out (lemma-level drift inventory + cited UR/QDA/Apple-HIG terminology research). Companion to [i18n-language-decisions.md](i18n-language-decisions.md) (the settled record) and `bristlenose/locales/glossary.csv` (the SSOT this proposes to extend).

The job: the 5 terms **Participant, Speaker, Tags, Theme, Friction** have no glossary row in any language except `ja`. This brief proposes targets for all 10 — plus normalizations for drift the research surfaced — so native UR reviewers can confirm before anything is written to the glossary or code.

Most apparent 'variation' in the locale files is **grammatical inflection, not drift** (`citação`/`citações`, `Sitzung`/`Sitzungen` = one word). After collapsing inflections, genuine lemma-level drift is modest and concentrated:

## Per-language native-reviewer questions (the 5 terms)

### cs — Czech
_Audited the Bristlenose cs (Czech) locale (9 JSON files) against the en source, decoding \u escapes so the Miro/desktop strings counted correctly. PART A — Of the 13 core concepts, only ONE shows genuine lemma drift: **Quote**, split between the dominant 'citát' (~88 inflected occ, used across the whole quote-browsing UI) and the alternate 'citace' (~17 occ, clustered in the Miro panel, the desktop quotes menu/hints, the settings configReference, and two stray actions in common.json — hideQuote/starQuote). The EN source uses the single word 'Quote(s)' for every one of those keys, so this is translator inconsistency, not a source distinction. 'citát' (quoted utterance) is the correct QDA/UX choice; 'citace' drifts toward 'bibliographic citation'. High severity — recommend normalising to 'citát'. The other 12 concepts are clean single-lemma: mluvčí (speaker), účastník (participant), téma (theme), štítek (tag), kód/kniha kódů (code/codebook), signál (signal), tření (friction), rámec (framework), hvězdička (star). Note 'rozhovor' (interview) vs 'sezení' (session) is NOT drift — EN itself distinguishes Sessions from Interviews and cs mirrors it faithfully; likewise 'redakce' (PII redaction feature) vs 'anonymizace' (export/backend) map to two distinct EN concepts. PART B/C — Researched the 5 unanchored terms with cited sources. Four already match the established Czech standard and should be anchored at HIGH confidence: účastník (academic ethics-aware UR norm; industry sometimes prefers 'respondent'), mluvčí (Azure Speech + Czech STT-tool convention; 'řečník' would be wrong register), téma (canonical Braun & Clarke CZ rendering). **Tags** is the one normalization flag (MEDIUM): Bristlenose uses 'štítky' but Apple's current macOS Czech localization uses 'značky' as the PRIMARY Finder/Mail Tags term (with 'štítky' as a noted synonym, 'tagy' in Reminders) — a HIG-alignment judgement call, not a correctness fix. **Friction** ('tření') is LOW confidence: the UX metaphor isn't conventionalised in Czech UX writing, so a native practitioner should confirm it reads naturally (alternatives: obtíže/překážky/frikce); it only appears in prose, so impact is minor. Proposed 6 glossary rows total (5 unanchored terms + the high-severity Quote drift). All sources cited; no terms invented; confidence lowered where authority was thin (Friction)._

- **Participant → `účastník`** (high, anchor): Bristlenose uses 'účastník' for every 'participant' string (a 1:1 match with the English source and the ethics-aware academic register). Czech industry UX writing frequently uses 'respondent' instead. Do you want to anchor 'účastník' (recommended — respectful, modern, matches source), or does your target audience expect 'respondent'? Confirm the genitive plural 'účastníků' and the nominative plural split 'účastníci' (animate) read correctly in the count strings.
- **Speaker → `mluvčí`** (high, anchor): Bristlenose uses 'mluvčí' for speaker (transcription/diarization sense), which matches the established Czech speech-tech and transcription convention (Azure, Czech STT tools).
