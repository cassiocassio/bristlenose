Observations

  • Circle background: --bn-colour-muted (#6b7280) is the natural choice — it's already the "secondary/quiet interactive" colour in the system (used by add-tag ghost badges, modal close, search clear text). Much softer than the current near-black --bn-colour-text.
  • Shadow: A single-layer 0 1px 3px var(--bn-colour-shadow) creates a subtle "floating chip" effect without drawing attention. The two-layer shadows (vars 4, 10, 11) create more depth but may be too prominent for something that only appears on hover.
  • Circle size: 0.75rem (12px) is tight but works at report scale. 0.875rem (14px) gives more breathing room for the × and looks less cramped, especially at the smaller margin-badge size. 1rem (16px, var 11) dominates the badge — probably too big.
  • × weight: Bold (700) reads clearly at 12px. Medium (500, vars 7-8) is more elegant but thinner strokes may look "spidery" at small sizes. Ultra-thin (400, var 12) depends heavily on font rendering and may vanish on low-DPI screens.
  • White circle: Vars 9–10 use a white bg with grey ×. These float visually but look less "button-like" — more like an annotation marker. They also need dark-mode consideration (would need to flip).
  • Centering: The current wonky alignment comes from font metrics of the × character combined with font-weight: 700. Using flexbox align-items: center; justify-content: center with consistent line-height: 1 fixes the centering across all variations shown here.
  • AI badge ×: The AI badge uses a ::after pseudo-element instead of a <button>. The same circle/shadow tokens should apply to both — define them as custom properties or share via a common class.
